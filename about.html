Rhoda Muse is a 5th year statistics PhD student at the University of Arizona minoring in Agricultural Resource Economics working with Dr Satheesh Aradhyula and Prof Ed Bedrick. My research involves accounting for endogeneity when quantifying the role of risk in agricultural technology adoption decisions. I simultaneously model yield and improved seed use, a binary variable and use the lagged yield and lagged yield variance to quantify risk. This is a departure from current empirical studies that have used two stage methods to quantify this effect ignoring the endogenous effect of the technology on yield moments. Both maximum likelihood and bayesian approaches are used. Models for discrete and continous data can account for endogeneity only, lagged effects only or heterosedascity but not the three issues simultaneously. We contribute to literature by jointly addressing the three issues.

Research Areas

  • Correlated binary and continous outcomes
  • Longitudinal data
  • Bayesian Statistics
  • Endogeneity
