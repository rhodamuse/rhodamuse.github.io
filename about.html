I am a 5th year statistics PhD student at the University of Arizona minoring in Agricultural Resource Economics. I am working with Dr Satheesh Aradhyula and Prof Joe Watkins. My research involves accounting for selection/endogeneity in a correlated binary and continous data model with lagged effects. This research is motivated by the role of production risk on the decision to adopt improved agricultural technology in East Africa. I simultaneously model yield and improved seed use, a binary variable and use the actual lagged yield, and lagged yield variance to quantify risk using both bayesian and maximum likelihood approaches. This is a departure from current empirical studies that have used two stage methods to quantify this effect ignoring the endogenous effect of the technology on yield moments. I also provide a solution to the initial conditions problem that arises when lagged or cross lagged effects are applied to short panels of data. We show with simulation that the proposed solution results in consistent estimates.

Research Areas
